## What IS Known (Literature-Documented)

### 1. Mattauch Isobar Rule (1934)

**Source**: [Wikipedia - Mattauch isobar rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mattauch_isobar_rule)

**Statement**: "Two nuclides that have the same mass number (isobars) can both be stable only if their atomic numbers differ by more than one."

**Violations**: Seven even-A values have THREE stable isobars:
- A = 96, 124, 130, 136, 148, 150, 154

**A=130 specifically** (Wikipedia):
- ¹³⁰Te (Tellurium-130)
- ¹³⁰Xe (Xenon-130)
- ¹³⁰Ba (Barium-130)

**Attribution**: Even-even pairing effects and N=78 shell structure

### 2. Valley of Stability

**Source**: [Wikipedia - Valley of stability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valley_of_stability)

**Definition**: "The valley of stability is a characterization of the stability of nuclides based on their binding energy, plotting by numbers of protons and neutrons."

**Decay Trajectories**:
- β⁻ decay: Neutron → proton (moves toward valley from neutron-rich side)
- β⁺ decay: Proton → neutron (moves toward valley from proton-deficient side)

**Qualitative statement**: "Decay pathways occur until daughter nuclei are on the Belt."

**What's missing**: NO quantitative analysis of WHERE products land.

### 3. Semi-Empirical Mass Formula (SEMF)

**Source**: [Wikipedia - SEMF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-empirical_mass_formula)

**Five terms**:
1. Volume term: 15.75 MeV per nucleon
2. Surface energy: Reduced binding for surface nucleons
3. Coulomb term: Proton-proton repulsion
4. Asymmetry term: (N-Z)² penalty
5. Pairing term: Even-even enhancement

**Predicts**: Nuclear masses and valley location (single curve)

**What's missing**: NO multiple bracketing curves. SEMF gives ONE stability line.

### 4. Beta Decay Systematics

**Source**: [European Physical Journal A - Beta-decay studies 2020](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1140/epja/s10050-020-00316-4)

**Focus**:
- Half-lives and decay rates
- Branching ratios
- Total absorption technique (Pandemonium effect correction)

**What's missing**: NO systematic study of product distributions relative to curves.

### 5. Nuclear Data Compilations
